Guillemette Schroepfer

My name is Guillemette Schroepfer and I am a Biomedical Engineering student double majoring in Religious Studies. In my free time I enjoy running, exploring new places outdoors, and trying new instagram recipes. Through the religious studies program I have learned about barriers religious people face within the current healthcare system, and I have learned there is not a one size fits all approach to problems relating to people. I have become passionate about inclusive design and ethical tech, and I am excited to explore these themes in this course. In Religious Studies this means thinking about decisions healthcare professionals can make, but I am excited to explore this more through design.

Why I took this course

Through my study of religious studies, I have developed a strong interest in the ethical implications of technology and how it can be designed to be more inclusive and equitable. I am particularly interested in how technology is often developed knowing that it will be used by a diverse range of people, but without fully considering the needs and experiences of those users. For religious people in particular, additional Electric Medical Records (EMRs) have been implemented to include a patient's religious affiliation, but often these lists are not inclusive of all religions and can have their religious needs overlooked. For example Epic Systems allows providers to record religious affiliations and create customized spiritual care workflows and assessment tools. This allows providers to assess care plans along with a patient’s spiritual practices. I think as AI gets integrated into this system which records patent conversations, and prepares summaries. Although Epic’s approach requires review of AI generated notes I am curious how the bias implemented by the computer may highlight the biases already present in our healthcare systems.
