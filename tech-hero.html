Sue Harnett

Founder & CEO of Rewriting the Code

Sue Harnett is the founder and CEO of Rewriting the Code. RTC is a global community of women in technology, designed to uplift women and address systemic barriers to entry and advancement in the tech industry.

In her own words, "when women are part of a strong community, they're more likely to persist, lead, and thrive."

With over 40,000 members, includying myself, her work and dedication to supporting young women in technology is felt globally. Her work has helped women feel empowered to continue their education and careers, and will continue to shape our industry.

Arion Long

Increasing access to organic period care through smart dispensers

Arion Long launched Femly after being diagnosed with a tumor linked with chemicals in period products she was using. Due to medical neglect she found herself on life support. Millions of black women are ignored by our healthcare system intersecting system racism and sexism. Through her lived experience as a black woman who was systematically ignored she founded Femly.

Femly's mission is to increase access to organic period care through smart dispensers that can be placed in schools and corporate restrooms around the country. Arion's work challenges norms in the tech space by addressing a need that is often overlooked and stigmatized: menstrual health. By creating innovative solutions for period care, Arion is advancing equity for people who menstruate, particularly those from marginalized communities who may face barriers to accessing traditional products. Sexism, Racism, and Classism affect women's menstrual experiences, by increasing access to safe products Femly hopes to change.

Joy Buolamwini

Founder of the Algorithmic Justice League

Joy Buolamwini is a researcher and artist who brought major attention to bias in facial analysis technology. Her work helped show how some systems performed worse for darker skinned faces and for women, which matters because these tools can influence access, safety, and opportunity in real life situations.

She founded the Algorithmic Justice League, an organization that pushes for accountability in automated decision making. A big part of her impact is translating technical problems into public conversations, so more people can understand what is at stake and ask better questions about how tools are built, tested, and used.

What makes her a strong tech hero for this course is that she challenges the idea that progress is only about faster and more powerful technology. She focuses on who gets included in data, who gets harmed by mistakes, and what it means to build systems that respect human dignity.
